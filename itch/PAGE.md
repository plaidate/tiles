# Tiles — four 1-bit games, built the NES/Game Boy way

**Tagline:** Four original 1-bit tile games for Playdate — bomb, shoot, quest, dig.

Tiles is a little collection of original games built the old way: a fixed
16-pixel tile grid, sprites and tilesets drawn entirely in code, and a
crank that does something different in every game. No two play alike —
one is a bomb-the-maze arcade, one a shrine-defense shooter, one a
screen-flip quest RPG, and one a scrolling dig-'em-up where gravity is out
to crush you. Every screen is code-drawn string art, so the whole thing is
tiny and runs cool on the hardware.

Each game is a self-contained take on a genre you already love, tuned for
short pick-up-and-play sessions and the Playdate crank. Grab the one that
sounds like your afternoon, or play all four — they share a look but not a
single mechanic.

## The games

- **Blast** — single-screen bomb-the-maze arcade (Bomberman-like). Trap
  the beasts in your blasts across five levels; the crank dials your fuse
  length. Chain bombs, grab powerups, don't blow yourself into a corner.
- **Spirit** — 4-way shrine-defense shooter (KiKi KaiKai-like). Hold the
  courtyard against eight waves of yokai with thrown talismans; spin the
  crank to bank a screen-clearing wand sweep.
- **Relic** — screen-flip action RPG (Zelda-like). Sword, keys, and a
  boomerang across a linked overworld and a four-floor dungeon down to the
  boss and the Relic. Saves as you go; the crank charges a spin attack.
- **Burrow** — scrolling Boulder-Dash-style digger. Tunnel a big cave,
  bank the gem quota to open the exit, and never stand under a falling
  rock. The crank pans the camera to survey the shaft.

## Features

- Four distinct games in one download, each a different genre
- 1-bit string art — every tile and sprite drawn in code, no external
  assets
- A unique crank mechanic per game (fuse dial, wand sweep, spin attack,
  camera pan)
- Corner-assist movement and a bold on-screen player marker so you never
  get lost in the dither
- Short, replayable sessions with fresh layouts (Burrow re-carves its
  caves every time)
- Save-and-continue in Relic

## Controls (quick reference)

- **Blast** — D-pad move, Ⓐ drop bomb, crank sets fuse length
- **Spirit** — D-pad move, Ⓐ throw talismans, crank spins to bank a sweep
- **Relic** — D-pad move, Ⓐ sword, Ⓑ boomerang, crank charges a spin
  attack
- **Burrow** — D-pad dig, crank pans the camera, Ⓐ start/continue

Full rules, enemy behaviors and tips for every game are in the manual.

## Install (no dev tools needed)

Each game is a separate Playdate app. Download the game's
`.pdx.zip` from the Releases page (or the `dist/` folder), then either:

1. Sideload it to your Playdate at
   [https://play.date/account/sideload/](https://play.date/account/sideload/), or
2. Unzip it and drop the `.pdx` into the Playdate Simulator.

No Playdate SDK or build toolchain required to play.
